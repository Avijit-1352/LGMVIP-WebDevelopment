Famous Style

Buzz Cut

The buzz cut is a masculine low-maintenance haircut for men who want really short hair. Stylish and easy to get, buzzed haircuts are often paired with faded or shaved sides. The hair cut close to the head with clippers doesn’t require any styling in the morning, and the cut can easily be done at home when it grows out a little longer than you’d like. A buzz cut hairstyle is an especially great option for guys with thick hair but can be a fashionable look if you have thinning hair or a receding hairline.

Mohawk

The mohawk is an edgy hairstyle with its origins in the punk rock movement. Mohawks feature a strip of long hair running from the hairline down to the back of the head with short or shaved sides. Traditionally, the long hair is styled into individual spikes, but there are many modern variations with soft and textured styling. The mohawk fade has become a popular look for guys who want an edgy style full of volume and flair.

Faux Hawk

Also known as a fohawk, the faux hawk is a trendy haircut style for guys who want to style a unique look. Simply put, the faux hawk hairstyle is a tame adaptation of the punk rock mohawk. The faux hawk starts with short hair on the sides and longer hair on top, which is pushed to the middle of the head and styled spiky. The faux hawk works well for men with straight full hair, which already tends to have volume. .

Pompadour

Pompadour is one of the most easily recognizable hairdos out there. We’ve written about it several times, and we’ve decided to give it the Haircut Inspiration treatment. The pompadour haircut has volume and length on top while the sides are short and slicked back. The cut was made famous by Elvis Presley but has evolved over time.
